# Problem Analysis
**Project:** DischargeWise
## Problem Statement

Hospital discharge is one of the most critical decision points in patient care. Before discharge, clinicians must evaluate large volumes of heterogeneous patient information—including diagnoses, laboratory results, medications, previous admissions, and clinical observations—to determine whether a patient can safely leave the hospital.

Although hospitals routinely collect this information, it is often distributed across multiple records and must be interpreted under significant time pressure. As a result, important combinations of risk factors may be overlooked, increasing the likelihood of preventable hospital readmissions.

The challenge is therefore not the absence of clinical data, but the difficulty of transforming complex patient information into timely, actionable insights that support safe discharge decisions.

---

## Current Clinical Workflow

A simplified discharge workflow typically follows these steps:

1. Patient reaches clinical stability.
2. The physician reviews the patient's clinical condition.
3. Laboratory results, medications, and medical history are assessed.
4. The physician determines whether discharge is appropriate.
5. The patient is discharged with follow-up instructions.

While this process is clinically appropriate, it depends heavily on manual review and individual experience.

---

## Pain Points

Several challenges make discharge decisions difficult:

- Large volumes of heterogeneous patient information.
- Limited time available for clinical review.
- Multiple interacting risk factors that are difficult to assess simultaneously.
- No simple way to prioritize the most relevant risk indicators.
- Limited decision support during discharge planning.

These challenges do not imply inadequate clinical expertise; rather, they reflect the complexity of modern healthcare environments.

---

## Opportunity

DischargeWise addresses these challenges by providing AI-assisted decision support during hospital discharge planning.

Rather than replacing clinician judgment, the platform analyzes available patient information, estimates readmission risk, explains the primary factors contributing to that risk, and presents the information through an intuitive clinical interface.

The final discharge decision always remains with the treating physician.

---

## Expected Impact

By supporting clinicians with transparent and interpretable risk assessment, DischargeWise aims to:

- Reduce preventable hospital readmissions.
- Improve the consistency of discharge planning.
- Increase clinician confidence during decision-making.
- Transform complex patient data into actionable clinical insight.
