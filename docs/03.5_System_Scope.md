# System Scope

**Project:** DischargeWise

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Approved

---

# Purpose

This document defines the scope and boundaries of the DischargeWise platform. It specifies what the system is designed to do, what it intentionally does not do, and the design philosophy that guides its development.

Clearly defining the system scope helps align stakeholder expectations, maintain a focused development process, and prevent unnecessary feature expansion.

---

# In Scope

DischargeWise is designed to:

- Predict the probability of 30-day hospital readmission.
- Classify patients into readmission risk categories.
- Explain the key factors influencing each prediction.
- Support clinicians during discharge planning.
- Present patient information through an intuitive clinical dashboard.
- Suggest workflow-oriented review actions before patient discharge.
- Improve the transparency and usability of AI-assisted clinical decision support.

---

# Out of Scope

DischargeWise does not:

- Diagnose diseases or medical conditions.
- Recommend or prescribe treatments.
- Recommend medications or dosages.
- Decide whether a patient should be discharged.
- Replace physician judgment.
- Modify electronic health records.
- Generate treatment plans.
- Operate as an autonomous clinical decision-making system.

---

# Design Principles

The following principles guide the design of DischargeWise:

## Human-Centered AI

The system supports clinicians rather than replacing them.

## Explainability First

Every prediction should be accompanied by understandable explanations.

## Progressive Disclosure

Only the most relevant information is shown initially, while additional clinical details remain available on demand.

## Human-in-the-Loop

The physician always retains full responsibility for the final discharge decision.

## Responsible AI

The system should promote fairness, transparency, privacy, and compliance with healthcare regulations such as GDPR.


# Success Criteria

DischargeWise is considered successful if it:

- Provides clinically meaningful readmission risk predictions.
- Explains predictions in a way clinicians can understand.
- Supports faster and more confident discharge planning.
- Integrates naturally into the clinical workflow.
- Assists decision-making without replacing physician judgment.
